For weddings with 80 to 120 guests, a realistic minimum budget often starts around $8,000 to $15,000 depending on venue, catering, and photography choices. Food and venue usually account for the largest share of the total wedding budget, often 50 to 60 percent combined. Photography and videography typically consume another 10 to 15 percent. Flowers, decor, and entertainment each account for roughly 5 to 10 percent. Couples should reserve 5 to 10 percent of their total budget as a contingency fund for unexpected costs. For smaller weddings of 40 to 80 guests, budgets of $5,000 to $10,000 are more feasible, though venue minimums may still apply. Budget overruns most commonly occur in catering, floral arrangements, and last-minute vendor additions. Setting a firm per-category budget early and tracking spending against it is the most effective way to stay on target.
