Guest count is one of the most significant cost drivers in a wedding. Every additional guest increases costs for catering, seating, invitations, favors, and venue size. Save-the-dates should be sent 6 to 12 months in advance for destination weddings and 4 to 6 months for local events. Formal invitations go out 6 to 8 weeks before the event with an RSVP deadline 3 to 4 weeks before the date. Expect 10 to 20 percent of invited guests to decline. A waiting list for additional guests can be activated once declines are received. Seating charts should be finalized no later than one week before the event to allow the caterer to prepare accurate meal counts. Meal choice cards included in invitations help caterers plan dietary accommodation quantities in advance.
