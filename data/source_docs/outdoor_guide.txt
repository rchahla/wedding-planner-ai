Outdoor weddings require weather backup planning regardless of the season. Spring and fall dates may introduce temperature swings, rain, or wind risks that affect guest comfort and decor stability. Summer weddings in hot climates require shade structures, fans, and chilled water stations for guest safety. Winter outdoor events are generally not recommended without substantial heated tent infrastructure. Tent rentals for 100 guests typically cost $1,500 to $4,000 depending on size and style, with additional costs for flooring, sidewalls, and climate control. Outdoor venues should be assessed for ground stability, as soft or uneven terrain can affect tent setup and guest mobility. Noise ordinances and curfews are common for outdoor venues and should be confirmed before booking. A formal weather contingency plan, including a named backup indoor space, should be established and communicated to all vendors at least two weeks before the event date.
