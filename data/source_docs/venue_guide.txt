Wedding venues typically require booking 9 to 18 months in advance for peak dates. Indoor venues offer climate control and are not affected by weather, making them a safe choice for winter or rainy-season weddings. Banquet halls, hotels, and event spaces often include tables, chairs, and catering infrastructure. Outdoor venues such as gardens, vineyards, and parks require rental coordination for furniture, lighting, and restrooms. Venue capacity must exceed guest count by at least 10 percent to allow for comfortable seating and movement. Most venues charge a flat rental fee plus a per-head catering minimum. Some venues have exclusive vendor lists, meaning couples must use approved caterers and photographers. Confirm noise curfews, parking capacity, and setup time windows before signing a venue contract.
