Catering plans should account for guest dietary restrictions such as vegetarian, vegan, halal, nut-free, and gluten-free needs. Final guest counts directly affect total catering cost, typically ranging from $50 to $150 per head depending on service style and menu complexity. Buffet service is generally less expensive than plated meals and offers more flexibility for dietary accommodations. Plated dinners require confirmed meal selections in advance, usually collected via RSVP meal choice cards. Caterers should be informed of all dietary restrictions at least four weeks before the event to allow for menu planning and sourcing. Bar service is often quoted separately and can add $20 to $60 per guest depending on whether it is beer and wine only or a full open bar. Cake cutting fees charged by the venue or caterer typically range from $2 to $5 per guest if an outside cake is brought in. Always request a tasting before signing a catering contract to confirm food quality matches the price point.
